I joined the Accelerator Neutrino Neutron Interaction Experiment (ANNIE) at Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory, US Department of Energy in 2015 as a young undergraduate Physicist with more ambition but expertise. I have managed to turn that around as a graduat student. This high-energy physics experiment is designed to study various interactions of an elusive particle known as netutrino. Given such sensitive measurements, where sigals are overdosed by the noise, our team designed and constructed a novel detector shown above and installed a novel picosecond photodetector known as the LAPPD.


Deep Learning

The power and prevalance of Deep Learning is everywhere from automated translations on your phone to search algorithm on your daily Google search. Deep Learning applications are limitless and its broad scope attracts people like me looking to expand on that ability to solve real world problems. Below is an illustration of an artificial neural network, inspired from the architecture of a human brain with neurons connected by some links that are able to transmit information between them. This particular illustration shows how a machine is able to understand and interpret a handwrriten digit.

My love for AI stems from such capabilities of Deep Learning; that is more than a machine that takes instructions and spits out an answer; that is able to intepret complex tasks - in this case interpreting uncharasmatic human handwritten digits.

This is an architecture of a Convolutional Neural Network. 

import tensorflow as tf

def generate_model():
model = tf.keras.Sequential([
    #first convolutional layer
    tf.keras.layers.Conv2D(32, filter_size=3, activation='relu'),
    tf.keras.layers.MaxPool2D(pool_size=2, strides=2),

    #second convolutional layer   
    tf.keras.layers.Conv2D(64, filter_size=3, activation='relu'),
    tf.keras.layers.MaxPool2D(pool_size=2, strides=2 ), 

    #fully connected classifier
    tf.keras.layers.Flatten(),
    tf.keras.layers.Dense(1024, activation='relu'),
    tf.keras.layers.Dense(10, activation = 'softmax') # 10 outputs
])

return model
