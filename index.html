Physics

In the summer of 2015, I joined the Accelerator Neutrino Neutron Interaction Experiment (ANNIE) at Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory, US Department of Energy; as a young undergraduate Physicist at 18, perhaps, with more ambition than expertise. I have, fortunately, progressed from that as a graduate student. This high-energy physics experiment is designed to study various interactions of an elusive particle known as netutrino with neutron. Given such need of high precision high sensitivity, where sigals are overdosed by the background noise, our team engineered a novel detector (the left image (during construction) - a 26 ton neutrino detector) and installed a novel picosecond photodetector known as the LAPPD, a first of its kind, and the most powerful, to date in the world, to measure at a picosecond time resolution.

My graduate thesis : Solving a physics problem using AI

In simple words, my research takes a fluid sample, shines a monochromatic Laser beam through the sample, collects data on the other end. Traditional approach includes collecting data, analyse each dataset, run simulations and conclude the water content/impurities. Using Convolutional Neural Network, I analyse the data frame-by-frame, run a CNN model with appropriate hyperparameteres and classify the water impuritiy at far lower computational cost and high accuracy. 

import tensorflow as tf

def generate_model():
model = tf.keras.Sequential([
    #first convolutional layer
    tf.keras.layers.Conv2D(32, filter_size=3, activation='relu'),
    tf.keras.layers.MaxPool2D(pool_size=2, strides=2),

    #second convolutional layer   
    tf.keras.layers.Conv2D(64, filter_size=3, activation='relu'),
    tf.keras.layers.MaxPool2D(pool_size=2, strides=2 ), 

    #fully connected classifier
    tf.keras.layers.Flatten(),
    tf.keras.layers.Dense(1024, activation='relu'),
    tf.keras.layers.Dense(10, activation = 'softmax') # 10 outputs
])

return model
